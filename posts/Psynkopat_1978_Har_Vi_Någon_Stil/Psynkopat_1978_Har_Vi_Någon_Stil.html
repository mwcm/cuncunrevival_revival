Psynkopat was obscure Swedish band, formed in 1978 by a merger of the groups Skunk and Max Scratching. The group started out playing what they themselves describe the hard-arranged music, written individually and collectively designed which can be heard on their LP. LP was released in May 1978 and in June the band went on tour.

Psynkopat played collectively improvised music with participation of the audience. Depending on the mood of the day, they played in minor or major key, and sometime they could play in four hours. In autumn 1978 the group stopped playing old composition and started to prepare special program for every new show. As a result, number of shows was extremely decreased, but shows themselves had more fun. Soon band was disbanded. Few band?s musicians later founded Tow Truck Two.

Primarily heavy progressive fusion mixed with experimental bits and a dollop of humor. If that sounds familiar, then yes, Psynkopat are indeed influenced by "Waka Jawaka" era Frank Zappa. The highlight of the album is the instrumental work, which remarkably manages to stay focused, with some fiery guitar - again emulating Zappa at his best. The sophomoric goofball elements drag it down considerably though. Other Scandinavian references are Storm (Sweden) and Dr. Dopo Jam (Denmark).
