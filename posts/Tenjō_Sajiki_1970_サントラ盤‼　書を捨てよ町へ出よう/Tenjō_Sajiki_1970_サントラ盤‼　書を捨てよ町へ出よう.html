Based on recordings of a live performance of 書を捨てよ、町へ出よう at Asagaya Public Hall.


Before "Throw Away Your Books" became the definitive film of Japan's late '60s generation, it was a hip off-Shinjuku play; and before the Tokyo Kid Brothers recorded their immortal film soundtrack, there was already a lesser-known play soundtrack done by Tenjo Sajiki.  While there is considerable overlap between the two (but featuring different musicians), there is also enough unique material to recommend each version.  Comparatively speaking, Tenjo Sajiki's approach is even more raw, innocent, and primitive.  Expect ego-dissolving garage rock, touching folk ballads about childhood, random found sounds, and lots and lots of strident political announcements.  A unique and thrilling release, that - along with the TKB version and Vangelis' Fais que ton rêve soit plus long que la nuit - is essential listening for anyone even remotely interested in the global youth rebellion of the late 60s, which was inspired in equal measure by Paris '68 and China's Red Guards.
