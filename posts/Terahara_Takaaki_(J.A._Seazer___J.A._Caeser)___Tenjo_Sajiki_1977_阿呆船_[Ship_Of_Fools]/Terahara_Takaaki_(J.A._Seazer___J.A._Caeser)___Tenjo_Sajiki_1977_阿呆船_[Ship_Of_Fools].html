In one of the ironies of history, the US-backed Shah of Iran - who was widely hated by his people due to his repressive policies - was a strong supporter of the global avant-garde, bringing in artistic heavyweights every year to his Shiraz Arts Festival.  At the 1976 festival Tenjo Sajiki debuted their new performance, Ahobune aka "Ship of Fools."  Who knows how it went down at the time, or if the troupe felt any sort of moral compunction at performing their work of Artaudian theater for a figure condemned by the left.  (Though perhaps there is poetic beauty in staging a "theater of cruelty" for a tyrant.)  The play itself, however, is fantastic, a stunning work of musical and emotional depth that is inferior to Jashumon and Shintokumaru only in the sense that "bronze" is inferior to "silver" or "gold."  For some reason, though, Ahobune has only rarely been reissued, and as a consequence remains relatively unknown within the mighty Seazer canon.

The most brilliant JA Seazer album.As most of his other albums in collaboration with Tenjo Sajiki's theater company which results usually with lots of spoken dialogs.Often his records can sounds boring during those parts,especially when you don't understand the Japanese language.By this 'Ship of fools' those spoken dialogs are limited in comparisation with his other albums.Lots of explosive parts with booming,rumbling bass explosion,wild choirs,crazied guitars,gongs,flutes...longer and more amazing than on his other albums.Takes you really to the throuth!Raritywise this is one of his most difficult to find records together with 'Baramon'.
