4 CD set. 60 Remastered tracks. Over 4¼ hours of music. Rare BBC archive recordings. Authoritative liner notes and band quotes.

The most comprehensive collection of the Pretty Things’ BBC ‘live’ recordings on the market, with several previously unreleased tracks, now all together for the first time on this newly remastered 4CD set, assembled with the assistance of the BBC.

Sourced from the BBC archives, this newly updated 60-track set covers numerous songs from the 1960s & 1970s period - including smash hits such as ‘Rosalyn’, Don’t Bring Me Down’, ‘Midnight To Six Man’, R&B standards ‘Road Runner’ and ‘Route 66’, and later classic album tracks ‘Defecting Grey’, ‘S.F. Sorrow Is Born’, ‘Singapore Silk Torpedo’ and ‘Dream / Joey’.

These sessions were recorded by the world renowned broadcasting organisation for radio and TV.

Our CDs explore all the Pretty Things’ BBC Radio One output, including 1960s ‘Top Gear’, ‘Saturday Club’, the later ‘Peel Sessions’ and ‘Sounds Of The 70s’, the ‘BBC In Concert’ series and legendary TV shows such as the ‘Old Grey Whistle Test’, amongst others.

The collection has been expertly remastered by Jon Astley (who has worked on recordings of The Who, Led Zeppelin, George Harrison and many others).

Detailed data in the booklets, that also include authoritative liner notes by Richard Morton Jack, editor of ‘Flashback’ magazine, and features new quotes from the Pretty Things Phil May and Dick Taylor.


Question: How do you take 8 newly unearthed tracks and spread them over 2 CDs to give the appearance of having doubled the length of the previously released BBC Sessions? Answer: Repeat 8 songs twice on the set. The BBC regularly re-aired previously recorded songs on subsequent programs. So by slyly listing them under the AIR date, Repertoire can claim different versions of identical performances.

On this set, Disc 2 Track 1 is a mono version of Disc 2 stereo Track 14. Disc 2 Track 5 is a repeat of Disc 1 Track 23. D2T6 is the same as D1T22. D2T9 is nearly identical to D2T2 – they must have been recorded on the same date. Disc 3 has an entire 5-song concert repeated with minor variations in the song introductions. And the track listed as Sweet Orphan Lady is actually Spider Woman from the Bouquets From a Cloudy Sky bonus material.

The songs do sound marginally better than the 2003 2-CD BBC Sessions, so kudos to Jon Astley for that. And it’s nice to have the goofy Brian Matthew intros intact this time. But all the redundancies are inexcusable. Did Jon really not notice when remastering that the underlying tracks were identical? Or is the deception intentional, to give the appearance of greater value?

The real shame is how much is still unreleased. The entire BBC In Concert show from November 28, 1974 at the Paris Cinema would have made this truly worthwhile. But they claim certain tapes still can’t be located. Maybe in another 10 years we’ll get an 8-CD set with another 10 new songs?
