Marià Albero began in the world of the song of author following in the footsteps of Raimon , recording an EP for the record Edigsa at the end of the 60s : Vam be tots to the newspaper of the tardor (1967), accompanied by Francesc Pi of the Serra on twelve-string guitar, he later recorded two simple records for the same company: Jo no ho crec / Classe mitja (1968) and La finestra / El joglar (1969). These first discs edited them with the name of Marian Albero.
Together with Manel Joseph (soloist of Orquesta Mirasol , Orquestra Plateria ) he formed in 1974 the ephemeral group Patatas fritas and recorded a simple record with Ariola , which would later give way to the formation La Rondalla de la Costa with which he would record the record Records de València (Zeleste / Edigsa, 1976) with Manel Joseph, Toni Xuclà , Martí Soler, Xavier García, Gustavo "Krupa" Quinteros and Xavier Batllés. With Manel Joseph will agree on later projects and maintain a good friendship.
In the 70's he produced albums such as Chichonera's cat (1975) by Ia & Batiste , a work that includes the theme "El gessamí i la rosa", a poem by Josep Carner, which is played by Clua and which Serrat includes in his Soundtrack d 'a temps d' a country .
Joan Manuel Serrat will record his friendship with Marià Albero in the dedication of the song Per al meu amic , brotherly song that dedicates to the Valencian friend and musician.
Marià Albero published his last album in 1995: Estampida , an album basically instrumental based on popular dances of diverse Mediterranean countries. Promoter and cultural activist, Marià Albero will later work on different fronts of the show, song, street theater and animation throughout his career. An artistic activity marked by enthusiasm and research work on the so-called root music.
