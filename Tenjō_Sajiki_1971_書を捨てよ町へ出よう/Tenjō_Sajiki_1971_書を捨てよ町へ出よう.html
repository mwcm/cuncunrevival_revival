Soundtrack album to the 1971 Shuji Terayama film, Sho O Suteyo Machi E Deyō.


First of all, I think its important to note that for the full impact of many of these songs, to experience them like you are supposed to, you should watch the film: 書を捨てよ町へ出よう [Throw Away Your Books, Rally in the Streets]. There are so many exhilarating, awe-inspiring sequences where both the audio and video is necessary to capture this image of Japanese counter-culture c. 1970, a wasteland of accelerated urbanisation, industrialization and alienated students branding sticks.

As for the album, as a standalone thing it sort of serves as something of a taster, an extract from this grand bizzare cinematic anti-masterpiece. Some of the slower, less upbeat songs don't work as well without context ad the more anthemic, energetic songs do on their own, and the language barrier (which isn't a problem in the film due to the subs) probably doesn't help. But the more anthemic tracks here are also really great on their own, which I can't really refer to by name but there's the one that as revealed by the film goes "YOUR LIBERATION - WHO IS IT FOR?" and some of those songs that really iconic to me and make me wish I could speak Japanese so I could sing a long to them or something

This is still rated 4 stars because I actually love the music in conjunction with the film and it deserves respect but if you liked this, then go and watch the movie how it should be experienced, get your mind blown, because you deserve it.

A beautiful & inspiring celebration of rebellion, at a time which much of the world's youth was eager to follow the example of China's Red Guards.  45+ years later, the heroes are no longer the same, but our collective need for genuine liberation - encompassing aesthetic, cultural, sexual, political, and ecological realms - remains.  Throw away the books; let's get out in the streets!
